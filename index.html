A private, AI-powered system that takes every document, photo, and record from your life — and makes all of it searchable, organized, and connected. Forever. On hardware you own.

The Problem

The paper pile never goes away

Physical documents buried in boxes, photos without context, and records held hostage by services you don't control. The information exists — you just can't reach it.

01 — Buried
The paper pile problem
Bills, medical records, contracts, tax docs — in boxes, in drawers, in filing cabinets nobody's opened in years. Impossible to find when you actually need them.
02 — Invisible
Photos with no context
Shoeboxes of photos from decades ago. No dates, no names, no way to search. Memories that exist but cannot be accessed.
03 — At risk
Cloud services you don't control
Existing solutions send your data to someone else's server. They can change their terms, raise prices, or shut down — and take your records with them.

How It Works

Scan it in, walk away

Five steps from physical paper to a structured, AI-readable archive. No manual filing. No decisions to make.

01
Capture
Scan a stack of documents with your phone. Auto-capture fires as you move pages — no buttons to tap.
02
Transfer
Files sync automatically from phone to your local machine over your home network. Nothing leaves your environment.
03
Read
OCR extracts text from every page. Documents with handwriting or stamps are image-preserved alongside the extracted text.
04
Classify
The AI reads the text, identifies what each document is, who it belongs to, and any action items that need attention.
05
Name & File
Every document gets a precise structured filename — findable by a human in Finder or an AI searching programmatically.

The Vision

The data you capture today gets more powerful every year

Every document you scan, every photo you ingest, every record you preserve — that's raw material. And raw material compounds.

The Shoebox
A photograph with no label. A family gathering, maybe the late 1970s. With enough compute, Strata looks at the clothing, the cars, the film grain, and says: this was probably 1978.
The Grandmother
Someone who passed years ago appears in a photo from 1965. Strata finds every image she ever appeared in — across decades, across shoeboxes you haven't opened in years. A life reconstructed from fragments.
The Diary
A diary written by hand in the 1990s. Strata reads it, indexes it, connects entries to photos from the same period. A timeline emerges — not just of paperwork, but of a life.
