The Data Layer

Scan it in, walk away

Five steps from physical paper to a structured, AI-readable archive. No manual filing. No decisions to make. Strata handles it — Bitty reads it.

01
Capture
Scan a stack of documents with your phone. Auto-capture fires as you move pages — no buttons to tap. Photos, handwritten notes, and physical mail all enter the same pipeline.
02
Transfer
Files sync automatically from phone to your local machine over your private home network. Nothing leaves your environment at any point in the process.
03
Read
Text extraction (optical character recognition) reads every word from every page using Apple Vision. Handwriting, stamps, and low-quality scans are image-preserved alongside the extracted text.
04
Classify
Bitty reads the extracted text, identifies what each document is, who it belongs to, and flags anything that needs attention — renewals, deadlines, action items.
05
Name & File
Every document gets a precise structured filename — findable by a human browsing their files on Mac or PC, or by Bitty searching programmatically. The filename is the filing system.

The Vision

The data you capture today gets more powerful every year

Every document you scan, every photo you ingest, every record you preserve — that's raw material. And raw material compounds. The archive you build now is the one a much more capable AI reasons over later.

The Shoebox
A photograph with no label. A family gathering, maybe the late 1970s. With enough compute, Bitty looks at the clothing, the cars, the film grain, and says: this was probably 1978.
The Grandmother
Someone who passed years ago appears in a photo from 1965. Bitty finds every image she ever appeared in — across decades, across shoeboxes you haven't opened in years. A life reconstructed from fragments.
The Diary
A diary written by hand in the 1990s. Bitty reads it, reasons across it, connects entries to photos from the same period. A timeline emerges — not just of paperwork, but of a life.
